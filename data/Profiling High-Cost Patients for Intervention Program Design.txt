Title: Profiling High-Cost Patients for Intervention Program Design

Scenario:
The leadership at a Rwandan health clinic has determined that a small percentage of patients account for a disproportionately high amount of healthcare costs. They want to launch a targeted intervention program to provide better proactive care for these individuals, but first, they need to understand who they are. You have been tasked with creating a detailed profile of these "high-cost patients."

Your Task:
Your objective is to identify the key clinical, demographic, and lifestyle characteristics that differentiate high-cost patients from the general patient population. 

Deliverables:
An analytical report or presentation, supported by data and visualizations, that provides the following:

Definition of a "High-Cost Patient": You must first define your methodology. For example, you could identify patients whose total costs fall in the top 20% (quintile) of all patients. Justify your choice.

Comparative Profile: Create a comparative analysis between your "high-cost" segment and the "non-high-cost" segment across several dimensions:

Clinical: What are the most common diagnoses? How many visits do they average? Which doctor specialties do they see most often?

Demographic: What is their average health_score? Which insurance_type and city are most common?

Wellness: How do their wellness habits (e.g., number of activities, types of activities, reported wellness_status) compare?

Key Findings Summary: A bulleted list of the 3-5 most significant factors that characterize a high-cost patient.

Recommendation: Based on your findings, what one or two factors should the new intervention program focus on first? (e.g., "The program should target diabetic patients in Kigali with low wellness engagement")


Skills Required:

Comprehensive Data Cleaning: Cleaning and preparing a multi-table dataset.

Data Merging & Integration: Joining all four tables to create a single patient-level view

Analytical Segmentation: Defining and creating a patient segment based on a calculated metric (total cost)

Feature Engineering: Creating aggregated metrics for each patient (e.g., total cost, visit count, wellness activity count)

Comparative & Exploratory Analysis (EDA): Using statistics and visualizations to uncover the key differences between patient groups

Synthesizing Insights: Translating analytical findings into a clear, actionable business recommendation.
