Title: Analyzing Monthly Trends of Flu Cases and Treatment Costs

Scenario:
The financial planning department at a Rwandan clinic is trying to forecast their budget for the upcoming year. 
They know that flu season significantly impacts costs, but they lack a clear, data-driven view of when the season peaks and how much it costs.

Your Task:
Your primary challenge is to perform a deep cleaning of the files. 
You must first create a reliable, standardized and columns. Once the data is trustworthy, you will analyze the monthly trends for all patient visits where the diagnosis was 'Flu'.


Deliverables:

A summary table that shows, for each month and year in the dataset:

The total number of flu cases recorded.

The total cost generated from treating those flu cases.

A brief written insight answering: "Which months represent the peak of the flu season in terms of cases and costs?"


Skills Required:

Advanced Data Cleaning: This is the core challenge. You must parse and standardize a wide variety of date formats (e.g., 'September 15, 2025', '2024/05/12', 'TT:04/22/2025') into a single, usable date format. You must also clean the cost column of non-numeric values and errors.

Date & Time Manipulation: You will need to extract the month and year from your newly cleaned date column to perform the monthly aggregation.

Time-Series Aggregation: Grouping cleaned data by a time period (month) to calculate summary statistics.

Filtering & Analysis: Isolating the data to only include 'Flu' diagnoses and interpreting the results to identify trends.
